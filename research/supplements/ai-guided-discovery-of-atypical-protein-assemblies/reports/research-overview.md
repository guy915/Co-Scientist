# **Research Overview: Defining a Structural Novelty Index (SNI) for Unconventional NRC-NLRs**

# **1. Summary of Main Research Directions**

The research goal is to move beyond sequence-based phylogenetics to define a structuralmechanical baseline for the NRC (NLR-required for cell death) family. The research is divided into five main directions:

1. **Quaternary Geometry and Oligomeric Symmetry:** Establishing the mathematical bounds of the canonical hexameric assembly using inter-protomer rotation and pitch.

- 2. **Activation Mechanodynamics (The NB-ARC Hinge):** Quantifying the "death-switch" transition by measuring the reorientation of the NB-ARC subdomains (NB, HD1, WHD).
- 3. **The MADA-Helix Pore Architecture:** Defining the structural requirements for membraneinsertion by measuring the tilt, protrusion, and amphipathicity of the N-terminal CCdomain.
- 4. **Interface Energetics and Solvation:** Assessing the "glue" that holds the resistosome together through Buried Surface Area (BSA) and hydrophobic density.
- 5. **Autoinhibitory Solenoid Curvature:** Mapping the LRR (Leucine-Rich Repeat) arc to understand how recognition surfaces deviate in unconventional variants.

# **2. Detailed Description of Main Research Directions**

### **I. Quaternary Geometry and Oligomeric Symmetry**

This direction focuses on the global arrangement of the 6-unit NRC resistosome. Canonical models (9FP6, 9RI9, 9CC8) show a highly symmetric, wheel-like structure. We will define parameters like the **Azimuthal Protomer Turning Angle (\$\theta\_{turn}\$)** and **Axial Stairstepping (\$\Delta z\$)**. By modeling the ~6,000 sequences into AlphaFold 3 (AF3) multimers, we can calculate how much a sequence "strains" against the hexameric constraint.

### **II. Activation Mechanodynamics (The NB-ARC Hinge)**

The NB-ARC domain acts as the molecular engine. In canonical NRCs, activation involves a massive rotation of the Winged-Helix Domain (WHD) relative to the Nucleotide-Binding (NB) slot. This direction utilizes the **HD1-WHD Pivot Angle (\$\Phi\_{hinge}\$)** to distinguish between "flipping" NLRs (canonical) and "non-flipping" or "stiff" variants that may function as decoys or sensors rather than executors.

### **III. The MADA-Helix Pore Architecture**

The N-terminal CC-domain contains the MADA motif, which forms the membranepermeabilizing pore. We focus on the **MADA \$\alpha1\$ Protrusion Height (\$H\_{\alpha1}\$)** and **Pore Constriction Radius (\$R\_{pore}\$)**. Deviations here indicate NLRs that might signal through protein-protein interactions rather than direct membrane lysis.

### **IV. Interface Energetics and Solvation**

The stability of the resistosome depends on specific contact points between the NB-ARC of one protomer and the LRR/CC of the next. We will quantify the **Interface Buried Surface Area (iBSA)** and **Hydrophobic Density (\$I\_H\$)**. This direction seeks to identify "symmetry breakers" where the interface is chemically incompatible with hexamerization.

### **V. Autoinhibitory Solenoid Curvature**

The LRR domain shields the NB-ARC in the inactive state. We will measure the **LRR Tangential Angle (\$\Omega\_{LRR}\$)** and solenoid radius. Unconventional NRCs often have truncated or extended LRRs, which alters the "reach" of the domain and its ability to maintain the "off" state.

### **Review Guidelines for the Structural Novelty Index (SNI)**

### **1. Factual Accuracy of Ground Truth Baselines**

- **PDB Consistency:** Reviewers must verify that the proposed numerical baselines for "canonical" structures match the actual data in PDB IDs 9FP6, 9RI9, and 9CC8. For example, 9RI9 is a resting-state dimer, not an active hexamer; using it as a reference for a "flat hexamer" metric is a fatal flaw.
- **The "Missing Residue" Problem:** Many experimental structures (like the ground truth set) lack coordinates for the N-terminal MADA motif (residues 1–15/20) because they are disordered. Ideas that propose measuring MADA "protrusion" or "solvent accessibility" against experimental PDBs as a baseline must explain how they account for these missing residues (e.g., using AF3-generated models of NRC2/3/4 as the true baseline).

#### **2. Technical Feasibility & Computational Scalability**

- **Tiered Screening Strategy:** Modeling 6,000 homo-hexamers in AlphaFold 3 is computationally prohibitive (thousands of GPU hours). A "good" idea must incorporate a phased approach—such as using monomeric metrics (mSNI) to filter the dataset before proceeding to expensive multimeric simulations (cSNI).
- **Memory and Token Limits:** Reviewers should flag ideas that ignore the hardware constraints of modeling large complexes (~5,000+ residues). Proposals must address whether the full sequence or truncated domains (e.g., CC-NB-ARC) are being used for the high-throughput phase.

#### **3. Mitigation of AI Modeling Bias ("Forced Folding")**

- **Detection of "Structural Frustration":** AlphaFold 3 may attempt to "force" a sequence into a hexameric shape even if the protein naturally prefers a different stoichiometry. An effective SNI must include metrics that detect this "strain," such as interface shape complementarity (\$S\_c\$), inter-protomer angular deviation, or axial "stair-stepping."
- **Stoichiometric Contrast:** High-quality ideas should suggest "contrastive modeling" comparing the confidence/fit of a sequence when modeled as a pentamer, hexamer, and heptamer—rather than assuming the hexameric model is biologically correct.

#### **4. Integration of Confidence Metrics**

- **Biological Signal vs. Computational Noise:** Geometric deviations (e.g., a \$15^\circ\$ shift in NB-ARC) are only meaningful if the model confidence is high. Reviewers must ensure the SNI incorporates AF3 confidence scores like **ipTM** (interface predicted Template Modeling) and **pLDDT**.
- **Penalty for Low Confidence:** A "novel" structure with low ipTM/pLDDT should be flagged as a potential modeling failure rather than a "novel" discovery.

### **5. Robustness of Automated Parameters**

- **Anchor-Based vs. Index-Based Numbering:** Plant NLRs are highly diverse with frequent insertions/deletions. Reviewers should reject ideas that rely on absolute residue numbering (e.g., "distance between Res 10 and Res 150"). Instead, parameters should be anchored to conserved motifs (e.g., P-loop, MHD motif, or the \$\alpha 1\$ hydrophobic core).
- **Geometric Nuance over Global RMSD:** Simple RMSD is too blunt a tool. Look for parameters that capture specific mechanistic shifts, such as the **NB-ARC swivel angle** (\$10^\circ\$ shift), the **protomer rotation angle** (\$60^\circ\$ for hexamers vs \$72^\circ\$ for pentamers), and **pore aperture radius**.

### **6. Functional Relevance of the CC-Domain**

● **Reference Set Stability:** Calculating Z-scores or Mahalanobis distances based on only 3–4 ground truth structures is statistically sensitive.
